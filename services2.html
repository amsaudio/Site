Mixing is the art and skill of creating space for different instruments, so that when they are added together they come alive and become more than the sum of their parts.

MIXING

Online/remote

Every mix is different. As an engineer, the artist's vision is the key aspect to understand and follow. Every song has an emotional charge and our task is to manipulate the tracks to amplify those emotions in a controlled way. This takes a trained ear and the right tools.
‍
A great mix will take your production to the next level, by giving it more power, depth and clarity.
‍
Some productions require heavy-handed work, a lot of cleaning up and processing. Others just need a little warmth and attenuation of certain frequencies.

I personally use a combination of digital plugins and analog gear in my mixes.
There are no rules - everything depends on the source material and aesthetic concept.

In the mixing stage, the engineer needs access to every track/instrument from the production.

MASTERING

Online/remote

Mastering is the last stage of post-production.
A good Master should be a non-destructive way to enhance a Mix to get it ready for distribution. Subtle coloring and processing might be added, depending on the source.
Most importantly, It will make the song sound loud enough for distribution, while still preserving the track's dynamic range.
On albums and EPs, this process aims to make the songs co-exist together in a natural way by shaping their levels, dynamics and frequencies.

STEM MIX / MASTER

Stem Mixing / Mastering is essentially the mixing and/or mastering of Groups of instruments.
Rather than working on all individual instrument tracks (mixing), the engineer works with a short numbers of groups, for instance: Drums + Bass + Vocals + Other.

This allows for some more flexibility to solve specific issues in the way the instruments blend together.
